STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE
Why video editing? Every YouTuber, startup, and business needs editors. The demand is insane and the barrier to entry is low. You can learn enough to earn in 2 weeks.
1

Learn One Tool (Free)

You only need ONE editing tool to start. Pick based on your device:

  • CapCut — Free, mobile-first, perfect for Reels/Shorts
  • DaVinci Resolve — Free, professional-grade, desktop
  • Canva Video — Free, easiest to learn, web-based

Watch 5 YouTube tutorials. Practice on 3 sample videos. That's enough to start.

2 weeks to learn
2

Build a Sample Portfolio

Edit 3-5 sample videos (use free stock footage from Pexels or Pixabay):

  • 1 YouTube-style talking head edit
  • 1 Instagram Reel with text overlays + transitions
  • 1 product showcase / promo video

Upload to Google Drive. Share link as your portfolio.

Show don't tell
3

Find Your First Client

Start local, then go online:

  • DM small creators (1K-10K followers) — "I'll edit one video free"
  • Fiverr / Upwork — create a gig, start at ₹500/video
  • Instagram DMs — reach out to local businesses
  • College events — offer to make aftermovies
First client in 1 week
4

Set Your Pricing

Start low, increase as you get testimonials:

  • Reels/Shorts: ₹500-1,500 per video
  • YouTube videos (10-20 min): ₹2,000-5,000
  • Ad films / promos: ₹5,000-15,000
  • Monthly retainers: ₹10,000-30,000 (4-8 videos/month)
Scale over time
5

Scale to ₹30K+/Month

Once you have 2-3 recurring clients:

  • Create packages — weekly or monthly bundles
  • Specialize — Reels editor, podcast editor, etc.
  • Raise rates every 3 months (market allows it)
  • Build a team — outsource basic edits, focus on premium
Long-term play
