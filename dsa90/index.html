Month 1 Foundations

Build your base. 2-3 problems daily. Focus on understanding, not speed.

Week 1-2: Arrays & Strings

Two pointers, sliding window, prefix sum, kadane's algorithm, string manipulation, anagram checks

Target: 25 problems

Week 3: Linked Lists

Singly & doubly linked lists, reversal, cycle detection (Floyd's), merge two sorted lists, find middle

Target: 12 problems

Week 4: Stacks & Queues

Stack using array/linked list, next greater element, valid parentheses, queue implementation, BFS prep

Target: 12 problems
Month 1 goal: Solve 50 problems. You should be able to solve any LeetCode Easy in under 20 minutes.
Month 2 Core Algorithms

This is where most people quit. Don't. 3 problems daily.

Week 5-6: Trees & Binary Search

BST operations, tree traversals (in/pre/post), level order, height, diameter, lowest common ancestor, binary search variations

Target: 25 problems

Week 7: Graphs

BFS, DFS, adjacency list/matrix, connected components, topological sort, shortest path (Dijkstra basics)

Target: 15 problems

Week 8: Dynamic Programming

1D DP (climbing stairs, house robber), 2D DP (grid paths, LCS), knapsack, memoization vs tabulation

Target: 15 problems
Month 2 goal: Solve 55 problems. You should be comfortable with LeetCode Medium problems (even if they take time).
Month 3 Interview Ready

Simulate real interviews. Speed + accuracy matter now.

Week 9-10: Advanced Topics

Heap/priority queue, trie, greedy algorithms, backtracking, bit manipulation, segment trees (optional)

Target: 20 problems

Week 11: Mixed Practice

Random LeetCode Medium problems across all topics. Simulate 45-minute interviews. Track time per problem.

Target: 15 problems

Week 12: Mock Interviews

Pramp (free), Interviewing.io (free), or practice with a friend. 2 mock interviews per week minimum.

Target: 4 mock interviews + 10 problems
Month 3 goal: Solve 45 problems + 4 mocks. You're interview-ready for most service and product companies.

Daily Routine Template

  • Morning (1 hr): Learn concept → watch video / read article
  • Afternoon (1.5 hrs): Solve 2-3 problems on that topic
  • Evening (30 min): Review solutions, note patterns
  • Total: 3 hours/day, 6 days a week (rest 1 day)

Platforms to Use

  • LeetCode — The gold standard (free tier is enough)
  • GeeksforGeeks — Great for concept explanations
  • NeetCode.io — Curated 150 problems with video solutions
  • Codeforces — For competitive programming practice
