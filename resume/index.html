ATS Cheat Sheet

Why 90% of resumes get rejected before a human sees them — and how to fix it

What is ATS?

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software that companies use to filter resumes BEFORE a recruiter sees them. If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, it gets auto-rejected — no matter how good your skills are. TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture — all use it.

DO's — ATS will LOVE your resume

  • Clean, simple format — no tables, no columns, no graphics inside the body
  • Standard section headings: Education, Skills, Experience, Projects (ATS looks for these exact words)
  • Keywords from job description — if they say "Python developer", your resume must say "Python"
  • Standard font: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, Times New Roman (10-12pt)
  • Save as PDF (unless they specifically ask for .docx)
  • Name & contact at TOP — not in header/footer (ATS can't read those)
  • Quantify achievements: "40% faster" beats "improved performance"

DON'Ts — ATS will REJECT your resume

  • NO Canva templates with columns, graphics, icons, or skill bars
  • NO images or photos — ATS can't read images at all
  • NO creative section names like "My Superpowers" instead of "Skills"
  • NO text inside text boxes — ATS skips them
  • NO headers/footers for important info — ATS ignores them
  • NEVER lie about skills — companies verify during interviews

The Keyword Hack

Open the job description. Copy the skills section. Match your resume wording to THEIR wording exactly. If they say "React.js", don't write "ReactJS". If they say "Machine Learning", don't write "ML". ATS matches EXACT keywords.
